      Practical Checklist

      Before you finalize your Seoul day plan

      Route Feasibility

      • Keep major stops in the same area when possible
      • Avoid stacking too many peak-time attractions
      • Leave one flexible slot for delays or queue time

      Hotel Decision

      • Choose by next-day route and transit convenience
      • Recheck check-in time and luggage policy
      • Balance rating with neighborhood access at night

      Food Planning

      • Save one backup restaurant for busy hours
      • Check opening day/hours before moving districts
      • Use budget mode to compare alternatives quickly

      On-the-Day Use

      • Save the route link before leaving Wi-Fi
      • Keep offline plan notes for fast reference
      • Adjust pace based on weather and group energy

      How to Use This Course

      This route is optimized for walkable movement with optional short subway transfers. A common flow is history or culture in the morning, shopping or cafe time in the afternoon, and local dining or views in the evening.

      Hotel and restaurant recommendations are prioritized using Google ratings and review volume, with additional curation shown as supporting context. Check map links for the latest opening hours, waiting time, and reservation availability.

      FAQ

      Planner questions from first-time visitors

      Can I use this planner for a half-day trip?

      Yes. Start with one style, then keep only the first 2-3 route blocks and one meal stop to build a shorter plan.

      Should I choose the hotel before the route?

      Usually no. Lock the main route first, then pick a hotel that reduces next-day transfers and late-night travel.

      Is this planner suitable for families or low-mobility travelers?

      Yes, but choose family or nature-oriented styles and remove extra walking stops. Keep longer rest windows between anchors.
